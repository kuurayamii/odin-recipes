Stroganoff - An Old King's Delight

An old King's delight, Stroganoff was a king who lost his teeth but his royal cook figured a way out for him to eat meat.

You can make any backing for this dish such as rice, chips, etc. Your choice!

Ingredients

  • 600g Fillet mignon or any tender meat.
  • 25g butter
  • 1 onion thinly sliced
  • 250g button mushrooms, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 400 ml beef stock
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 to 3 heated tbsp of cream, sour cream or creme fraiche
  • Chopped parsley, to serve
  • salt to taste
  • Ground pepper to taste

Steps

  1. First prepare the beef fillet. Cut it into slices ½–1 cm thick, then slice these into strips about 1cm wide. Season the meat with salt and pepper and set it aside for a few minutes.
  2. Heat the butter in a large frying pan. Add the onion and sauté for 2 minutes, then add the mushrooms and continue to cook until both are soft. Stir the mustard into the pan, coating the onion and mushrooms thoroughly.
  3. Pour the stock into the pan, then leave it to simmer until the liquid has reduced by about half. Stir in the cream and set the pan aside for a few minutes.
  4. In another large frying pan, heat the vegetable oil. When it’s smoking hot, add the strips of beef. Fry, stirring continuously, until the meat is browned on all sides. This should take about a minute at the most.
  5. Reheat the onion and mushroom sauce, then add the beef. Check the seasoning and add more salt and pepper to taste. If you find the sauce too rich, add a squeeze of lemon. Sprinkle with parsley before serving.
